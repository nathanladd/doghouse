Quick Start — Step by Step
1
Install Batteries
Install 5 AAA batteries in the handheld display unit.
2
Position the Scale
Set the scale on a firm, level surface.
3
Connect Display
Pull out the spiral cable from the side of the scale and connect the handheld unit.
4
Power On
Press POWER — the buzzer will beep once when the self-test is complete.
5
Select Unit
Press UNIT to select your preferred unit (Kg, Oz, or Lb).
6
Tare the Scale
Press ZERO to tare the scale.
7
Place Tank
Place the refrigerant tank in the center of the platform.
8
Tare Tank Weight
Press ZERO again to tare out the tank weight — now the display shows only refrigerant weight.
9
Monitor
Monitor weight in real time during charging or recovery.
How the Features Work
⚠️
Over-Range Warning

If weight exceeds 100 kg / 220 lb, the screen shows "FULL" and the red LED flashes. Remove weight immediately to avoid damaging the platform and sensor.

💤
Auto Power Off

If the weight on the platform doesn't change for 10 minutes, the scale shuts off automatically to save battery.

🔄
Power-On Self-Test

Runs automatically at startup — takes less than 2 seconds.

⚖️
Tare Function

Works across the full 100 kg range — you can tare out a heavy tank and still read the net refrigerant weight accurately.

Safety & Care
⚠️ Do's and Don'ts

✅ Use the scale only within the specified temperature and humidity range
✅ Replace batteries before the display becomes unstable — a bad reading at the wrong time can throw off a charge
✅ Remove batteries if storing the scale for an extended period — leaking batteries can corrode the internals

❌ Do not use chemical solvents or corrosive liquids to clean the scale
❌ Do not disassemble the scale body or internal components — this will cause weighing errors
❌ Do not yank or stretch the spiral cable — this can damage the sensor and throw off accuracy
❌ Do not exceed 100 kg / 220 lb on the platform

💡 Pro Tip for Techs

Always tare the scale with the tank on it before opening valves — that way you're reading net refrigerant flow, not guessing from a starting weight.
